# About.

This is an essential project.

The number of adults whose nervous systems are running too hot is no longer a fringe statistic. It is the baseline state of working life in most developed economies. Cognitive load is at historic highs. Sleep is collapsing. Anxiety is rising across every age cohort. The structures that used to provide coherence — stable careers, predictable institutions, intelligible economies, durable identities — are dissolving faster than most people can adapt to.

The work here is the response to that.

To put one practical instrument — the breath — into the hands of people who need it, and to do so without any of the things that would normally come with a wellness brand: no community, no streaks, no gamification, no infinite optimization, no constant nudging.

A book. A weekly letter. The Return app.

The intent is that more people can live more coherently in a destabilizing time. That is the whole purpose.

## Christoph Kneip

I spent roughly a decade as head of procurement at a multinational, working in environments where the cognitive load was high and the nervous system cost was higher. Outside of work, I have been a sustained student of contemplative practice — particularly the geometric and harmonic frameworks of Robert Edward Grant, and the question of what breath actually *does* at the physiological and structural level.

The book *Return to Breath* came out of the intersection of those two lives. The Return app — and this site — are the practical extensions of it.

I do not consider myself a teacher in the traditional sense. I consider myself someone who paid close enough attention to the breath, for long enough, to be able to translate what is happening into language that overstimulated adults can actually use.

If anything here lands, it is because the work has been done in private long before it arrived in public.

Based in Zürich, Switzerland.

## Rickard Strongert · Contributor

Rickard is a post-identity coach based in Marbella, Spain. He works with people who are at the threshold between an identity that no longer fits and the next form that has not yet arrived — founders post-exit, professionals navigating AI displacement, people in retirement, midlife transitions, late-career pivots.

His writing on this site addresses the question that opens every collapse of identity: *Who am I when I am not what I do?*

The collaboration is intentional. The book and the app are about the breath as a physiological instrument. Rickard's contribution is about the breath as a *holding ground* — what to do when the structures of self temporarily dissolve and you are asked to exist without them.

These are two halves of the same practice.

## What this site is, and is not.

This site is a publication. It is the writing arm of the Return practice. New work appears here weekly. The Return app is the practice arm — quiet, contained, and built around the same principles.

This site is *not* a community. There is no forum, no comments, no feed. Your relationship to this work is your own. We will not ask you to share, post, streak, or invite friends. You are welcome to do those things on your own. We are simply not optimizing for them.

If the writing is useful, you can subscribe to the weekly protocol. If it is not, you can ignore it. Either is fine.

We trust the people who arrive here to know what they need.
