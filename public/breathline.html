Where in your system — your life, your identity, your choices, your relationships — is there still unresolved motion?

What pattern is still cycling without settling?

What has not yet crossed into coherence?

Phase 1 — Scan the field

Close your eyes and take 3 full breath cycles (inhale, hold on top, exhale, hold at the bottom), emphasising the pauses.

Then ask yourself at the breathline (hold on top and hold at the bottom):

"What in my life is still reaching?"

Don't answer with your mind. Let the body or a sensation or an image respond.

Ready?

Phase 2 — Name it without story

Once something surfaces, resist the urge to explain or justify it.

Just name it cleanly.

Phase 3 — Locate it in the breath

Now breathe with this unresolved pattern in your awareness.

Notice: Where in the breath cycle does it create tension?

Does it spike during the inhale? (grasping, taking in, accumulating)
Does it grip during the top pause? (holding, controlling, not releasing)
Does it resist during the exhale? (fear of letting go, scarcity)
Does it collapse during the bottom pause? (emptiness feels threatening)

The unbreathed parts will distort the natural rhythm when you bring them into awareness.

Where does yours live in the cycle?

Ready?

Phase 4 — Breathe it into stillness

Once you've located where it grips:

Deliberately bring your attention to that phase of the breath.

E.g. if it grips on the exhale:
Slow your exhale — let the release be complete — breathe through the resistance.

If it grips at the pause:
Extend the pause slightly — stay with the discomfort — let the breathline hold it until it settles.

Do 9 cycles with this specific attention and intention.

You're not forcing resolution. You're breathing the pattern into the breathline — into the place where coherence can operate on it.

Extend the gripping breath phase to a suitable length for you (between 5–9 sec) and click Start to breathe your 9 cycles.

Phase 5 — Notice what releases or clarifies

After the 9 cycles, return to natural breathing.

Ask at the breathline (hold for a few seconds again on top or at the bottom):

"What has shifted?"

Not "is it solved?" but "what's different now?"

Stay a moment in stillness and observe your state.

Thank you
